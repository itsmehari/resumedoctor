# ATS-Friendly Resume: Complete Guide for 2026

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software used by employers and job portals to screen resumes before a human sees them. In India, platforms like Naukri, LinkedIn, Indeed, TimesJobs, and Shine use ATS or similar technology. If your resume isn't formatted correctly, it may never reach a recruiter.

## What is an ATS?

An ATS parses your resume into structured data—name, email, skills, work history, education—and ranks it against the job description. Resumes that score well move forward; others get filtered out. The system looks for:

- **Keywords** from the job description
- **Standard section headings** (Experience, Education, Skills)
- **Clean formatting** without tables, columns, or graphics
- **Readable fonts** and consistent structure

## How to Make Your Resume ATS-Friendly

### 1. Use Standard Section Headings

Stick to common headings recruiters and ATS expect:

- **Professional Summary** or **Summary**
- **Work Experience** or **Experience**
- **Education**
- **Skills**
- **Projects** (if relevant)

Avoid creative headings like "Where I've Been" or "My Journey." ATS may not recognize them.

### 2. Include Relevant Keywords

Read the job description carefully. Include industry terms, tools, and skills mentioned in the posting. Use them naturally in your summary, experience bullets, and skills section. Don't stuff keywords—that can backfire.

### 3. Avoid Complex Formatting

- No tables or text boxes
- No multi-column layouts
- No headers and footers for critical content
- No images or graphics (except a simple profile photo if required)
- Use common fonts: Arial, Calibri, or similar

### 4. Save in the Right Format

When applying online, use **PDF** if the portal allows. Ensure the PDF is text-based, not a scanned image. Some older ATS prefer Word (.docx). Check the job posting for preferences.

### 5. Spell Out Acronyms Once

Write "Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech)" the first time, then use "B.Tech" later. This helps both ATS and human readers.
