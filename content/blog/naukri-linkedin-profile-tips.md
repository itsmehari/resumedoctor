Your resume gets you the interview — but your online profile gets you found in the first place. Indian recruiters use both Naukri and LinkedIn extensively. Here's how to maximise both.

## Naukri Profile Optimisation

Naukri's algorithm ranks profiles based on **profile completeness** and **keyword density**. A complete, keyword-rich profile gets surfaced to recruiters automatically.

### Priority Checklist

**1. Resume Headline (most important)**  
Your headline appears in every recruiter search result. Keep it specific:

✅ `Full Stack Developer | React, Node.js, 4+ Years`  
❌ `Looking for opportunities`

**2. Key Skills Section**  
Add all relevant skills explicitly — don't assume the algorithm will extract them from your resume. Naukri's search uses the skills field heavily.

**3. Current and Desired Locations**  
Set both. Recruiters filter by location first.

**4. Salary Expectations**  
Set a realistic range. Profiles with salary info get more recruiter visibility.

**5. Resume Freshness**  
Naukri's algorithm prefers recently updated profiles. Re-save your profile at least once a month even with no changes — this bumps your ranking.

**6. Notice Period**  
Set accurately. Many recruiters filter on notice period.

## LinkedIn Profile Optimisation

LinkedIn is used more by MNCs, startups, and tech companies. It's also the primary channel for inbound recruiter outreach.

### The Big Five

**1. Professional Photo**  
Profiles with photos get 14× more views. Use a clean headshot with a neutral background. No selfies or cropped group photos.

**2. Headline**  
By default, LinkedIn uses your job title. Customise it to include keywords:

`Senior Backend Engineer @ Razorpay | Python · Go · Distributed Systems`

**3. About Section**  
This is your professional summary expanded to ~150 words. Write in first person. Include:
- What you do and for how long
- Your top 2–3 specialisations
- A notable achievement
- What you're open to

**4. Experience with Bullets**  
Don't just list your job title and company. Add 3–5 achievement-focused bullet points per role (same as your resume).

**5. Skills + Endorsements**  
Add your top 10 skills. Ask colleagues to endorse the key ones. LinkedIn shows skill endorsements in search results.

### Open to Work Flag

If you're actively job hunting, turn on **"Open to Work"**. You can set it to show only to recruiters (not your current employer's network) by selecting **"Recruiters only"** in the settings.

### LinkedIn vs Naukri: When to Use Which

| Platform | Best for |
|---|---|
| **Naukri** | Mid-size Indian companies, IT services, BPO, mass recruitment |
| **LinkedIn** | MNCs, startups, senior roles, international opportunities |
| **Both** | Best results – keep both active |

## Consistency Between Resume and Profiles

Recruiters cross-check your resume with your Naukri/LinkedIn profile. Inconsistencies (different dates, different job titles) are a red flag.

**Rule:** Your resume, Naukri, and LinkedIn should tell the same story with the same dates and titles.
